Welcome!

My name is Bochen Yang,
a master's student in Post-Soviet Public Policy

Currently, I am pursuing my graduate studies under the supervision of Associate Professor Maksim Vilisov, where I am conducting research on the ongoing war between Russia and Ukraine. My research focuses on determining whether the war is a doomed one or an artificial crisis through the lens of hostile perception, and a range of qualitative methods will be employed to achieve this goal. One of the primary sociological methods I am using is conducting elite interviews with individuals from both Russia and Ukraine, and foreigners who have extensive knowledge and/or experience on this matter. Through these interviews, I hope to gain a deeper understanding of the underlying ideological factors driving the decade-long conflict since 2013 and eventually causing the full-blown war, as well as potential pathways towards resolution. I am also analyzing a range of secondary sources, including academic literature, news articles, and government reports, to develop a comprehensive understanding of the conflict.

Prior to my current research, I conducted an investigation into Russia's strategic utilization of passportization and its compatriots residing in other post-Soviet countries as a means of achieving favorable geopolitical outcomes, with a particular emphasis on the 2014 Ukrainian Crisis. Additionally, I examined the public discourse conflict between Russia and Ukraine in the international arena. My research endeavors have also extended to Central Asia, specifically Turkmenistan, where I explored the challenges and opportunities associated with the country's natural gas exportation in light of the Russo-Ukrainian conflict and the global trend towards decarbonization. This research culminated in my Bachelor's thesis.

I am also seeking a PhD position in Political Science and/or Public Policy now, with a particular interest in Peace Studies and Ideology in the post-Soviet space. I am enthusiastic about contributing to cutting-edge research in this field and am eager to explore any opportunities that may be available. Please do not hesitate to contact me if you would like to discuss my qualifications further.
